Your Personality is ENFP!

Extraversion iNtuition Feeling Perceiving

ENFPs or Campaigners are known to be some of the most charismatic and lively of people. They are kind and idealistic people-pleasers. ENFPs see life as a big puzzle - a puzzle of emotion and deeper meaning as opposed to merely a series of factual jigsaw pieces. They don't take things at face value and aren't afraid to question authority. ENFPs are very sociable beings and they are missed when not at parties. They love to meet new people and learn about different experiences and perceptions of the world.

Strengths vs. Weaknesses

Strengths

- Curiosity: ENFPs like to experience new things, explore the unknown and learn. They are open-minded so almost no adventure is off-limits and they have usually have no problem leaving their comfort zone in order to pursuit these new and exciting experiences.

- Observation: Everything is relevant. Campaigners believe that any small action can effect the outcome in a significant way. They are firm believers of the "Butterfly Effect" and this means they don't dismiss any detail. It also means that they have good lateral-thinking abilities as these fine details aren't disregarded when problem-solving.

- Social Skills: ENFPs tend to be highly social. As a result, they are great communicators, know a wide range of people and are quite enthusiastic. As they tend to talk to a lot of people, they have a wonderful ability to empathise with situations that don't necessarily relate to them - chances are they know someone that it does relate to and have heard the effects something's had on them.

Weaknesses

- Highly Emotional: Emotions are what make us human. However, Campaigners can have mood swings or be prone to stress as a result. They can unknowingly take things out on others which will strain relationships. Additionally, their current mood can significantly affect their self-esteem. They could be frustrated by a difficult problem which may cause them to evaluate their self-worth based on their ability to solve the issue.

- Overthinking: As they tend to look into the deeper meaning of situations, ENFPs can tend to find issues that aren't necessarily there. To them, there is more often more than meets the eye to a situation, but they're not always right. This can result lead to worrying about issues that may not exist.

- Poor Concentration: This can also be considered to be the downfall of the ENFP's curiosity. They spend time daydreaming about the "what-ifs" and can lose sight on the task at hand. They affinity for thinking about new possibilities and things that mystify them is something that is not all too helpful when there's a deadline at midnight tonight.

Careers

Campaigners tend to prefer a "laissez-faire" type fo career, meaning that they tend to like jobs in which they have freedom to be creative and can express their ideas. They are explorers, who want to push the boundaries in both their personal and professional lives. Repetitive jobs do not suit ENFPs - they want variation and to try new things.

A career like journalism, reporting, or even theatre and acting in the media industry may satisfy the desire of ENFPs to discover interesting things and avoid monotony.

Relationships

Due to their social nature and deep-thinking, ENFPs can get quite involved in the lives of others, and are prone to putting others ahead of themselves. The love they have for their friends, family and partners can also have its consequences. They're idealistic way of thinking can be a cause for naivety - expecting the best from people in a world where life is often unfair. This may cause strain in a relationship when they find out the other person may not be as perfect as they had hoped.
