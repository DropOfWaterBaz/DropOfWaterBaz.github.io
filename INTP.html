Your Personality is INTP!

Intraversion iNtuition Thinking Perceiving

INTPs or Logicians are curious and have a very creative imagination. They tend to lose themselves in their daydreams and get tired easily from socialising. They are high-energy introverts and connect well with those who match their mental energy.

Strengths vs. Weaknesses

Strengths

- Objectivity: Logicians primary goal is the truth. They look deeper into things and want to find out about any underlying realities. This means that they are a good control and are very honest.

- Interesting: INTPs have a large foundation of knowledge and can make great conversation about wide variety of interesting topics.

- Open-Mindedness: In search of the truth, INTPs are accepting of different ideas, insights and methods as long as they are back-up by good reasoning.

Weaknesses

- Social Unawareness: As they let their minds wander so often, INTPs can also let this happen when in social situations. This can leave the people with them feeling ignored and unheard.

- Perfectionism: Logicians spend a lot of time about what could be better. This can apply a lot to their personal ideals but also to their work, which can lead to them finding themselves frustrated.

- Impatience: When speaking about their ideas or thoughts, INTPs have a tendency to get frustrated when others can't keep up with a topic and may dismiss the person they're talking to.

Careers

Logicians are exploratory people. They like to delve into a project and figure out how to overcome the obstacles that come with it. They can understand intricate and complex systems.

Although they don't have bad people-skills, logicians may prefer less customer-oriented positions. They are innovative and like structure in their work. As they can recognise multiple opportunities and foresee problems, a career in a planning position such a corporate strategist would be well-suited to an INTP.

Relationships

As logicians quite like their time alone, they are quite selective about who they do spend their time with. They tend to become friends with people who share their love for new theories and riddles. They are not against having people challenge their ideas, however.
